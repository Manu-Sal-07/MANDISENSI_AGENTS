--- FRAME 4: Problem Statement

\begin{alertcard}{\faIcon{exclamation-circle}~The Problem}
\scriptsize
Indian mandi prices are \textbf{volatile and unpredictable} due to weather, seasonal gluts, and policy shifts. Current tools are \textbf{monolithic, static, and opaque} --- providing no reliable or explainable price intelligence.
\end{alertcard}

\vspace{0.04cm}

\begin{columns}[T]
\begin{column}{0.48\textwidth}
\begin{purplecard}{\faIcon{crosshairs}~Scope \& Constraints}
\scriptsize
\begin{itemize}\setlength\itemsep{1pt}
\item Multi-agent mandi-level forecasting
\item Volatility + regime detection
\item Explainable via SHAP/LIME
\item Cross-commodity spillover
\item \textbf{7-day ahead}, daily granularity
\end{itemize}
\end{purplecard}
\end{column}
\begin{column}{0.48\textwidth}
\begin{successcard}{\faIcon{rocket}~Expected Outcomes}
\scriptsize
\begin{itemize}\setlength\itemsep{1pt}
\item \textbf{MAPE $<$15\%} across regimes
\item Real-time $2\sigma$ deviation alerts
\item SHAP driver attribution
\item Reduced farmer uncertainty
\item Scalable across commodities
\end{itemize}
\end{successcard}
\end{column}
\end{columns}

--- FRAME 9: Literature Synthesis: Key Findings

\begin{columns}[T]
\begin{column}{0.48\textwidth}
\begin{successcard}{\faIcon{thumbs-up}~Strengths \& Trends Observed}
\footnotesize
\begin{itemize}\setlength\itemsep{2pt}

\item \textbf{T2:} GARCH volatility clustering (\textbf{91\%} acc.)
\item \textbf{T3:} Transformers beat RNNs on long range
\item \textbf{T4:} XAI (SHAP/LIME) boosts trust
\item \textbf{T5:} ICEEMDAN/VMD effective denoising
\end{itemize}
\end{successcard}
\end{column}
\begin{column}{0.48\textwidth}
\begin{alertcard}{\faIcon{thumbs-down}~Critical Limitations Identified}
\footnotesize
\begin{itemize}\setlength\itemsep{2pt}
\item \textbf{22/25} use \textbf{monolithic} pipelines
\item \textbf{0/25} use \textbf{adaptive} ensemble weights
\item Volatility = \textbf{output}, not predictive signal
\item XAI \& forecasting in \textbf{separate silos}
\item \textbf{Zero} cross-commodity modeling
\end{itemize}
\end{alertcard}
\end{column}

--- FRAME 10: Research Gaps Identified
\vspace{-0.1cm}
\begin{columns}[T]
\begin{column}{0.48\textwidth}
\begin{tcolorbox}[enhanced, colback=accentred!4, colframe=accentred!40, arc=5pt,
  boxrule=0.3pt, left=5pt, right=5pt, top=2pt, bottom=2pt,
  shadow={0.5pt}{-0.5pt}{0pt}{black!8}]
\fontsize{6.5pt}{7.5pt}\selectfont
\textbf{\color{accentred}\faIcon{cube}~Gap 1: Monolithic Architectures}\\
Single-model pipelines cannot model heterogeneous market drivers independently.
\end{tcolorbox}
\vspace{0.02cm}

\begin{tcolorbox}[enhanced, colback=accentorange!4, colframe=accentorange!40, arc=5pt,
  boxrule=0.3pt, left=5pt, right=5pt, top=2pt, bottom=2pt,
  shadow={0.5pt}{-0.5pt}{0pt}{black!8}]
\fontsize{6.5pt}{7.5pt}\selectfont
\textbf{\color{accentorange}\faIcon{balance-scale}~Gap 2: Static Ensembles}\\
Fixed weights treat stable markets and crises identically---unreliable when accuracy matters most.
\end{tcolorbox}
\vspace{0.02cm}

\begin{tcolorbox}[enhanced, colback=accentpurple!4, colframe=accentpurple!40, arc=5pt,
  boxrule=0.3pt, left=5pt, right=5pt, top=2pt, bottom=2pt,
  shadow={0.5pt}{-0.5pt}{0pt}{black!8}]
\fontsize{6.5pt}{7.5pt}\selectfont
\textbf{\color{accentpurple}\faIcon{chart-line}~Gap 3: Volatility as Afterthought}\\
Volatility treated as output metric, not integrated into forecasting logic or adaptive weighting.
\end{tcolorbox}
\end{column}

\begin{column}{0.48\textwidth}
\begin{tcolorbox}[enhanced, colback=accentblue!4, colframe=accentblue!40, arc=5pt,
  boxrule=0.3pt, left=5pt, right=5pt, top=2pt, bottom=2pt,
  shadow={0.5pt}{-0.5pt}{0pt}{black!8}]
\fontsize{6.5pt}{7.5pt}\selectfont
\textbf{\color{accentblue}\faIcon{random}~Gap 4: No Regime Awareness}\\
Models assume stationarity; fail to detect structural breaks from monsoon surplus or festival spikes.
\end{tcolorbox}
\vspace{0.02cm}

\begin{tcolorbox}[enhanced, colback=softcyan!6, colframe=softcyan!50, arc=5pt,
  boxrule=0.3pt, left=5pt, right=5pt, top=2pt, bottom=2pt,
  shadow={0.5pt}{-0.5pt}{0pt}{black!8}]
\fontsize{6.5pt}{7.5pt}\selectfont
\textbf{\color{softcyan}\faIcon{eye-slash}~Gap 5: No Explainability}\\
Black-box outputs erode trust; no driver attribution or interactive interpretability.
\end{tcolorbox}
\vspace{0.02cm}

\begin{tcolorbox}[enhanced, colback=accentgreen!4, colframe=accentgreen!40, arc=5pt,

  shadow={0.5pt}{-0.5pt}{0pt}{black!8}]
\fontsize{6.5pt}{7.5pt}\selectfont
\textbf{\color{accentgreen}\faIcon{link}~Gap 6: No Cross-Commodity Analysis}\\
All models forecast single commodities; ignore spillover and substitution effects.
\end{tcolorbox}
\end{column}
\end{columns}

--- FRAME 23: Why Multi-Agent Forecasting?
\vspace{-0.2cm}

\begin{columns}[T]
    % Left Column: Monolithic
    \begin{column}{0.48\textwidth}
        \begin{alertcard}[height=5.2cm]{\faIcon{times-circle}~~Traditional Monolithic Forecasting}
            \fontsize{7pt}{8.5pt}\selectfont
            \begin{itemize}\setlength\itemsep{4pt}\setlength\parskip{0pt}
                \item \textbf{Signal Dilution}: A single model attempting to capture seasonality, supply stress, and news anomalies simultaneously suffers from parameter conflict.
                \item \textbf{Regime Blindness}: Static architectures fail to capture non-linear market shifts (e.g., sudden export bans or monsoon failures).
                \item \textbf{Error Propagation}: A single raw feature anomaly (e.g., reporting error in arrivals) poisons the entire forecasting pipeline.
                \item \textbf{Rigid Constraints}: Inability to integrate heterogeneous data types (e.g., daily prices + weekly weather + text news) without leakage.
            \end{itemize}
        \end{alertcard}
    \end{column}
    
    % Right Column: Multi-Agent
    \begin{column}{0.48\textwidth}
        \begin{successcard}[height=5.2cm]{\faIcon{check-circle}~~Multi-Agent Market Intelligence}
            \fontsize{7pt}{8.5pt}\selectfont
            \begin{itemize}\setlength\itemsep{4pt}\setlength\parskip{0pt}
                \item \textbf{Isolated Specialization}: Decomposes market intelligence into independent, domain-specific expert agents.
                \item \textbf{Driver Separation}: Seasonality, volume elasticity, and external events are modeled independently without interference.
                \item \textbf{Fail-Safe Redundancy}: A failure or anomaly in one stream (e.g., news API) is isolated, leaving core forecasts active.
                \item \textbf{Adaptive Fusion}: Confidence-aware consensus weighting dynamically reroutes authority based on current market states.
            \end{itemize}
        \end{successcard}
    \end{column}
\end{columns}

--- FRAME 25: Seasonality Agent Architecture
\vspace{-0.2cm}

\begin{columns}[T]
    % Left Column: Process Flow
    \begin{column}{0.48\textwidth}
        \begin{infocard}{\faIcon{history}~~Temporal Inference Pipeline}
            \fontsize{6.8pt}{8.5pt}\selectfont
            \begin{enumerate}\setlength\itemsep{3pt}\setlength\parskip{0pt}
                \item[\textbf{1.}] \textbf{Cycle Isolation}: Historical price sequence ($P_t$) decomposed into trend ($T_t$), seasonal ($S_t$), and residual ($I_t$).
                \item[\textbf{2.}] \textbf{STL Decomposition}: LOESS-based smoothing isolates calendar frequencies (e.g., 30d harvest cycles).
                \item[\textbf{3.}] \textbf{Multiscale Lags}: Generates lookback features from $T_t$ and $S_t$ across multiple windows ($1d, 7d, 14d, 30d$).
                \item[\textbf{4.}] \textbf{Forecasting Model}: Trains Ridge Regression models on decomposed components to predict future cycles.
            \end{enumerate}
        \end{infocard}
    \end{column}
    
    % Right Column: Architecture Specifications
    \begin{column}{0.48\textwidth}
        \begin{smallcard}[0.98\textwidth, top=3pt, bottom=3pt, left=4pt, right=4pt, boxsep=0.5pt]
            \centering \textbf{\textcolor{primaryDark}{\small \faIcon{cogs}~~Mathematical Pipeline}}
            \vspace{0.05cm}
            \fontsize{6.8pt}{8.2pt}\selectfont
            \begin{itemize}\setlength\itemsep{3.5pt}\setlength\parskip{0pt}
                \item \textbf{Additive Model}: 
                \[ P_t = T_t + S_t + I_t \]
                Extracts clean seasonal indices.
                \item \textbf{Feature Lags}: Builds auto-regressive matrices on $T_t$ and $S_t$, neutralizing noisy daily fluctuations.
                \item \textbf{Regularization}: L2 Ridge penalty ($\lambda = 1.0$) prevents overfitting to short-term volatility.
                \item \textbf{Predictive Scope}: Extrapolates robust 30-day cyclical trends as crop pricing baselines.
            \end{itemize}
        \end{smallcard}
    \end{column}
\end{columns}

--- FRAME 27: Arrival Volume Agent Pipeline
\vspace{-0.2cm}

\begin{columns}[T]
    % Left Column: Pipeline Steps
    \begin{column}{0.48\textwidth}
        \begin{infocard}{\faIcon{truck-loading}~~Supply Ingestion \& Elasticity}
            \fontsize{6.8pt}{8.5pt}\selectfont
            \begin{enumerate}\setlength\itemsep{3.5pt}\setlength\parskip{0pt}
                \item[\textbf{1.}] \textbf{Arrival Ingestion}: Tracks daily incoming truckloads ($Q_t$) across primary agricultural markets.
                \item[\textbf{2.}] \textbf{Volume Profiling}: Standardizes raw arrival numbers into historical z-scores to isolate seasonality.
                \item[\textbf{3.}] \textbf{Elasticity Estimation}: Computes log price-to-volume rolling elasticity to predict supply responsiveness.
                \item[\textbf{4.}] \textbf{Shock Generation}: Maps supply anomalies directly to short-term price adjustments.
            \end{enumerate}
        \end{infocard}
    \end{column}
    
    % Right Column: Rationale
    \begin{column}{0.48\textwidth}
        \begin{smallcard}[0.98\textwidth, top=3pt, bottom=3pt, left=4pt, right=4pt, boxsep=0.5pt]
            \centering \textbf{\textcolor{primaryDark}{\small \faIcon{balance-scale-right}~~Supply-Side Price Elasticity}}
            \vspace{0.08cm}
            \fontsize{6.8pt}{8.2pt}\selectfont
            \begin{itemize}\setlength\itemsep{4pt}\setlength\parskip{0pt}
                \item \textbf{Why it exists separately}: Price cycles tell only half the story. Sudden supply shortages or glut arrivals break seasonal patterns.
                \item \textbf{Elasticity Metric}:

            \fontsize{6.8pt}{8.5pt}\selectfont
            \begin{enumerate}\setlength\itemsep{3.5pt}\setlength\parskip{0pt}
                \item[\textbf{1.}] \textbf{Ensemble Output}: Captures the primary consensus forecast ($F_{raw}$) from Phase-1.5.
                \item[\textbf{2.}] \textbf{Residual Estimation}: Trains a localized Ridge model to learn structural error bias ($e_t$) of the ensemble.
                \item[\textbf{3.}] \textbf{Dynamic Blending}: Combines the raw prediction and estimated residual correction using a time-varying $\alpha$:
                \[ F_{final} = F_{raw} + \alpha(t) \cdot e_t \]
                \item[\textbf{4.}] \textbf{Stabilization}: Dampens forecast spikes, resulting in a smooth, low-variance final path.
            \end{enumerate}
        \end{infocard}
    \end{column}
    
    % Right Column: Rationale
    \begin{column}{0.48\textwidth}
        \begin{smallcard}[0.98\textwidth, top=3pt, bottom=3pt, left=4pt, right=4pt, boxsep=0.5pt]
            \centering \textbf{\textcolor{primaryDark}{\small \faIcon{compress-arrows-alt}~~Why a Second Correction Stage?}}
            \vspace{0.08cm}
            \fontsize{6.8pt}{8.2pt}\selectfont
            \begin{itemize}\setlength\itemsep{4pt}\setlength\parskip{0pt}
                \item \textbf{Systematic Bias Reduction}: Primary ensembles often over-predict price peaks. Residual training isolates and subtracts this recurring skew.
                \item \textbf{Dynamic $\alpha$-Scaling}: In high-volatility regimes, $\alpha \rightarrow 1.0$ (maximum correction). In stable regimes, $\alpha \rightarrow 0.0$ (trusts raw baseline).
                \item \textbf{Error Spikes Reduced}: Reduces extreme forecast errors by over 22% during peak seasonal transitions.
            \end{itemize}
        \end{smallcard}
    \end{column}

--- FRAME 75: Key Findings \& Empirical Insights
\vspace{-0.2cm}

\begin{columns}[T]
    \begin{column}{0.48\textwidth}
        \begin{successcard}[height=5.2cm]{\faIcon{check-circle}~~Empirical Key Takeaways}
            \fontsize{6.8pt}{8.5pt}\selectfont
            \begin{itemize}\setlength\itemsep{4.5pt}\setlength\parskip{0pt}
                \item \textbf{Decomposition Benefits}: Splitting price streams into trend and noise cycles prevents model overfitting to sudden local noise.
                \item \textbf{Ensemble Superiority}: Adaptive weighting out-performed single networks by 18--25\% during extreme market shifts.
                \item \textbf{Causality Boost}: Integrating cointegration parameters from related commodities resolved lag issues during seasonal turnarounds.
            \end{itemize}
        \end{successcard}
    \end{column}
    
    \begin{column}{0.48\textwidth}
        \begin{infocard}[height=5.2cm]{\faIcon{lightbulb}~~Operational Takeaways}
            \fontsize{6.8pt}{8.5pt}\selectfont
            \begin{itemize}\setlength\itemsep{4.5pt}\setlength\parskip{0pt}
                \item \textbf{Decision vs Prediction}: Providing clear advisories (Buy/Wait) rather than raw numbers significantly improved usability.
                \item \textbf{Risk Gating Works}: Active GARCH bounds prevented over-purchasing during supply bubbles.
                \item \textbf{Low-Latency Core}: Redis-backed caching and WebSockets are critical to support live, multi-user trading desks.
            \end{itemize}
        \end{infocard}
    \end{column}
\end{columns}
